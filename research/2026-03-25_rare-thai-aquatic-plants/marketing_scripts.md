# Marketing Scripts — Rare Thai Aquatic Plants
> Marketing Agent output | 2026-03-25 | Format: TikTok / IG Reels

**Guiding principle:** Storytelling-first. A specific character in a specific moment that makes the viewer feel the problem. No pitch language. No "introducing." No "link in bio" energy.

---

## Script 1: The Grab Driver's Salad
**Character:** Somchai, 54-year-old Grab driver who thinks every green leaf is either for soup or "herbal relaxation"

**Hook (0–3s)**
*(Close-up of a tiny shimmering purple leaf in a plastic bag)*
"Somchai thinks I'm running a very strange, very expensive salad delivery service."

**Story (3–30s)**
*(Cut to Somchai holding the bag with two fingers, looking suspicious)*
We're idling in Sukhumvit traffic. He looks at this tiny clump of *Bucephalandra Ghost 2011* and asks if it's for soup. I tell him it costs ฿400. He laughs because his lunch cost ฿50. He doesn't know that in a shop in Brooklyn, this exact "soup ingredient" is sitting in a high-tech LED tank with a $160 price tag. To him, it's a weed that grows on rocks in the jungle. To a guy in New York, it's the holy grail of his aquarium.

**Reveal (30–40s)**
*(Cut to the plant being placed into a professional shipping crate)*
This is the Wednesday morning ritual at Chatuchak. Finding the "soup" before the rest of the world realizes it's actually jewelry for fish tanks.

**Soft CTA (40–45s)**
*(Text overlay: "The price of 'salad' in Bangkok vs. Brooklyn.")*
Just another Wednesday in the humidity.

**Captions**
- Somchai was unimpressed by my $160 "salad."
- One man's jungle weed is another man's centerpiece.
- The 13x markup that Somchai will never understand.

**Comment bait:** Would you pay $160 for a plant the size of a thumb?

## Script 2: The Ratchaburi Grandma's Footbath
**Character:** Mae Pranee, grandmother in Ratchaburi with a rare plant "infestation" clogging her drainage canal

**Hook (0–3s)**
*(Mae Pranee scrubbing laundry next to a patch of rare plants)*
"Mae Pranee thinks these plants are annoying because they clog her drain."

**Story (3–30s)**
*(Camera pans to thick carpet of *Anubias 'Pinto'* — white and green variegated leaves)*
These are rare mutations. In London, a single 3-inch pot goes for $180. Here in Ratchaburi, Mae Pranee is hacking them back with kitchen scissors because they're "getting in the way of the soap suds." She offers me a whole bucket for the price of a Thai tea. She thinks she's winning the deal.

**Reveal (30–40s)**
*(Cut to plants being cleaned and prepared for export)*
I'm not just buying plants — I'm saving Mae Pranee's drain and sending a piece of the Thai jungle to a guy in Germany who's been on a waiting list for six months.

**Soft CTA (40–45s)**
*(Sun setting over the farm)*
Shipping the "weeds" of Ratchaburi to the world.

**Captions**
- Grandma's garden waste is actually a goldmine.
- The $180 plant that's currently clogging a drain in Thailand.
- Why Ratchaburi is the secret capital of the aquarium world.

**Comment bait:** What's the most expensive thing you've ever seen someone treat like trash?

## Script 3: The Ari Hipster's Overkill
**Character:** Punn, 24-year-old Ari district barista who spends his whole paycheck on aesthetic "vibes"

**Hook (0–3s)**
*(Punn misting a plant with a vintage brass sprayer)*
"Punn bought a $110 plant to match his $800 espresso machine. He has no fish."

**Story (3–30s)**
*(Camera zooms in on *Bucephalandra Skeleton King* sitting in a glass jar on the counter)*
He calls it "minimalist decor." I didn't have the heart to tell him that three hours ago I bought its twin brother near the border for $8. He's talking about the "textural complexity" of the leaves while I'm thinking about the 12x price gap between this coffee shop and the muddy bank where it was pulled from.

**Reveal (30–40s)**
*(Box being labeled for Global Express Shipping)*
The world wants the Ari vibe but doesn't want to get their boots muddy to find it. That's where I come in.

**Soft CTA (40–45s)**
*(Punn sipping coffee in the background)*
Keeping the aesthetics high and the sourcing direct.

**Captions**
- ฿280 at the source. $110 on the coffee bar.
- Punn doesn't have a fish tank but he has the Skeleton King.
- Sourcing the texture that everyone is chasing.

**Comment bait:** Do you buy plants for the vibe or for the biology?

## Script 4: The 4AM Fever Dream
**Character:** Dave, sleep-deprived American tourist who wandered into Chatuchak on a Wednesday morning by accident

**Hook (0–3s)**
*(Dave looking blurry-eyed and sweaty as a vendor shoves a bag of moss at him)*
"Dave just wanted a souvenir. He accidentally found a $200 rarity."

**Story (3–30s)**
*(Handheld shaky market-cam footage)*
It's 4:15 AM. Humidity at 98%. Dave is holding a clump of *Eriocaulon quinquangulare* — the King of Moss. The vendor is shouting "5 dollars!" because he wants to go home and sleep. Dave thinks it's a cool souvenir. If Dave takes this through customs, he's carrying a plant that hobbyists in California would trade their firstborn for.

**Reveal (30–40s)**
*(Narrator's hand reaches in and buys the plant)*
Dave didn't buy it. I did. I already know which tank in Ohio this is going to.

**Soft CTA (40–45s)**
*(Market lights flickering off)*
The best deals happen when the world is still asleep.

**Captions**
- 4 AM at Chatuchak is a different dimension.
- The $5 "souvenir" that's worth $200 in California.
- Don't sleep on Wednesday mornings in Bangkok.

**Comment bait:** Have you ever found a treasure at a market completely by accident?

## Script 5: The Customs Officer's Confusion
**Character:** Officer Kiat, who has processed 4,000 shipments today and just wants his Pad Thai

**Hook (0–3s)**
*(Officer Kiat looking through a magnifying glass at a tiny root)*
"Officer Kiat thinks I'm smuggling dirt. I'm actually shipping a mortgage payment."

**Story (3–30s)**
*(Close-up of *Cryptocoryne Silver Queen* — looks like a sad, wet silver leaf)*
He's looking at the paperwork. It says "Aquatic Plant." He looks at the plant. It looks like something he stepped on in the parking lot. He asks why someone in France is paying so much for "muddy leaves." I try to explain aquascaping but he just sighs and stamps the box. He doesn't see the silver shimmer — he just sees a reason to delay his Pad Thai.

**Reveal (30–40s)**
*(Box sealed with Passed tape)*
That "muddy leaf" is a $90 specimen that grows in exactly one creek in Southern Thailand. By Friday it'll be the centerpiece of a gallery in Paris.

**Soft CTA (40–45s)**
*(Box sliding down a conveyor belt)*
From the mud to the world.

**Captions**
- Officer Kiat just wants his lunch. I just want my Crypts.
- Shipping "dirt" to Paris.
- One man's muddy leaf, another man's $90 masterpiece.

**Comment bait:** What's the hardest thing you've ever had to explain to a customs officer?
